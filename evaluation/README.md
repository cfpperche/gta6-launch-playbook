# Evaluation

The job of this file: turn 12 opportunities into a **shortlist of 1–2 to pursue**.
It does that in three parts — a fixed rubric, a first-pass scorecard, and a
decision shortlist. The scorecard is deliberately incomplete: the intrinsic
columns are filled, the personal-fit columns are not, because they need the
owner's inputs (see § *What's needed to finish this*).

Scores run **1 (worst) to 5 (best)**.

---

## 1. Rubric

Two groups of dimensions.

### Intrinsic — scored objectively (done)

| Dimension | 1 | 5 |
|---|---|---|
| **Capital safety** | needs heavy ongoing capital | needs almost none |
| **Downside safety** | total loss of money possible | only time is lost |
| **Defensibility** | trivially copied, no moat | durable moat (skill barrier, brand, community lock-in) |
| **Upside ceiling** | modest side income | genuinely large outcome |
| **2026-window fit** | cannot launch in the 2026 window (needs ~2027 PC port) | launches cleanly into the launch window |

### Personal-fit — needs the owner's input (pending)

| Dimension | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **Skill fit** | how well the opportunity maps to the owner's *existing* strengths |
| **Time fit** | the owner's realistic weekly hours vs. what the opportunity demands |
| **Risk-appetite fit** | the owner's tolerance for variance/loss vs. the bet's profile |

**Final score = intrinsic average + personal-fit average.** Until the personal-fit
side is filled, intrinsic is only a *filter*, not a verdict.

## 3. Decision shortlist (provisional — intrinsic-only)

Provisional because personal-fit is still missing. Stated as a recommendation the
owner can redirect.

### Primary candidate — #6 "Picks & shovels"

Best risk-adjusted option and the most robust to *whatever* the personal inputs
turn out to be: top intrinsic score (4.2), bounded downside (worst case is time,
not money), low capital, and it sells **before, during, and after** launch rather
than depending on one launch-day moment. It is also the natural **funder and
de-risker of #7** — a working tooling catalogue proves demand and generates the
capital a server would need. Strong default regardless of the inputs below.

### Conditional candidate — one of the creator cluster (#2 / #1 / #3 / #4)

These four are really **one bet**: "can the owner build and hold creator
attention." Worth picking *at most one* (with #3+#4 as a combinable pair) — and
**only if skill fit is genuinely high**. If the owner is not already strong on
camera or in content, their high intrinsic score is misleading and they should be
cut. This is the slot the personal inputs will decide.

### Phase-2 candidate — #7 RP server

Highest ceiling on the board, but capital- and risk-gated and timing-gated to the
~2027 PC port. Best treated as a *phase 2*: run #6 first; if it validates demand
and throws off capital, #7 becomes a funded, de-risked follow-on rather than a
cold high-capital bet.

### Cut from the build shortlist

- **#5 In-game AI NPCs** — real moat, but 2027-gated and a narrow B2B market.
  Keep as a watch item, not an active build.
- **#12 Web3 RP server** — highest combined barrier and probably ToS-blocked.
  Parked pending the Rockstar-ToS signal (see [`../timeline/`](../timeline/)).

### Tier 2 — a separate, capped decision

Not a substitute for a Tier 1 build. If pursued at all: a fixed, small,
pre-decided share of capital; TTWO (#8) is the only item with a business beneath
it; memecoins (#10) are strictly money-you-can-afford-to-lose. Sizing and entry
are a capital-allocation conversation, tracked in [`../timeline/`](../timeline/),
not a reason to skip building.

## 4. What's needed to finish this

The shortlist above is intrinsic-only. To lock it, four owner inputs are needed —
they fill the `?` columns and resolve the conditional candidate:

1. **Skills** — what are your genuine strengths? (writing, on-camera, design,
   software/dev, AI tooling, business/sales, community). This scores **skill fit**
   per opportunity.
2. **Time** — realistic hours per week you can commit between now and launch.
   This scores **time fit**.
3. **Capital** — how much, if any, you would put at risk. This gates #7 and the
   entire Tier 2 question.
4. **Risk appetite** — appetite for variance: steady-but-modest, or
   high-variance-high-ceiling. This scores **risk fit** and breaks ties.

Once supplied, the personal-fit columns get scored, the final ranking is computed,
and the shortlist collapses from "provisional" to a locked 1–2.

> **Methodology note.** Intrinsic scores are a first-pass judgement, not gospel —
> challenge any cell that looks wrong. Personal-fit scores are the owner's, not
> the analyst's. The rubric exists to make the reasoning legible, not to
> mechanize the decision.
