# Automation brief — 4. AI coaching & strategic guides

> **Opportunity:** be first to publish data-driven GTA 6 guides at launch,
> capturing the "GTA 6 + [anything]" search spike. Catalogue:
> [`../opportunities/README.md`](../opportunities/README.md).
> **This brief:** how far AI takes development + operation toward 100%.

## Verdict — ~80%

Content *production* is ~90% automatable. Two things hold the ceiling down:

1. **The raw data.** Real money-per-method, mission times, optimal builds — these
   require *actually playing GTA 6 at launch*. An agent cannot play a console game
   on day one. The data must be human-played or sourced from those who do.
2. **The 2026 SEO wall.** Programmatic/template SEO is dead — the March-2026
   penalty wave cut scaled thin content 60–90%. Only genuinely unique,
   E-E-A-T-backed content ranks, and "ranking" now also means being *cited by
   ChatGPT / Gemini / Perplexity* (answer-engine optimization).

## Build phase — automating development

- Site + content infrastructure: agent-built (static site, CMS, analytics) —
  trivial for agentic coding.
- Pre-build the content skeleton: the agent drafts every "GTA 6 + X" guide
  template *in advance*, ready to fill with real data on launch day.
- AEO/keyword map: an agent researches the full "GTA 6 +" query space.

## Operate phase — automating execution

- **Launch sprint:** as gameplay data arrives, agents fill templates and publish
  same-day at volume.
- An **AEO agent** optimizes for ChatGPT/Gemini/Perplexity citation, not just blue
  links.
- An **update agent** keeps guides current as the in-game meta shifts.
- A **monetization agent** manages affiliates and ads.

## The AI stack (2026)

Agentic coding (Claude Code) for the site · research + AEO agents · Claude writer
agents · fact-check agent · genuinely-unique-content programmatic-SEO platforms ·
analytics.

## Human-in-the-loop — the supervisor role

- **Generate or source the real data** — play the launch, or partner with players.
  This is the irreducible input the whole operation depends on.
- **E-E-A-T & accuracy** — wrong guides destroy both trust and rankings; human
  verification is mandatory in 2026.
- **The credible brand/face** the guides are attributed to.

## What blocks the last mile to 100%

An agent cannot play a freshly launched console game. 2026 SEO explicitly requires
human-verified value. Trust attaches to a credible author.

## Failure modes of the automated approach

Publish fast but wrong → ranking and reputation collapse together. Publish generic
AI guides → penalized as scaled thin content. Miss the launch-week window → the
traffic spike is gone (this is a *timing* play). If GTA 6 data-mining and PC tools
lag, verified data is scarce exactly when the window is open.
