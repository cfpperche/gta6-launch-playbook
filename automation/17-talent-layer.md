# Automation brief — 17. Talent & labor layer

> **Opportunity:** a staffing/marketplace or training-and-certification business
> for the GTA 6 creator economy — the editors, RP writers/actors, moderators,
> community managers, server admins the economy will demand. Catalogue:
> [`../opportunities/README.md`](../opportunities/README.md).
> **This brief:** how far AI takes development + operation toward 100%.
> **Tier 3** — a blind-spot addition that *hedges* the automation thesis; see
> [`README.md`](README.md).

## Verdict — ~70% (the least automatable of the 17 — by design)

Fittingly, the opportunity whose *product is human labor* is the hardest to
automate. The marketplace mechanics — matching, scheduling, payments, vetting
workflow — automate; course content automates. But the core asset is a curated
pool of *trusted humans* and the relationships on both sides, and that is
structurally a people-business. This is also the one opportunity that **gains if
the automation thesis underperforms** — it is the playbook's hedge.

## Build phase — automating development

- Agentic coding builds the marketplace platform (or the course/certification
  platform) end to end.
- Content agents draft the training curriculum and certification materials.

## Operate phase — automating execution

- Matching agents pair talent to clients; screening agents run first-pass vetting;
  billing and scheduling agents handle operations; a support agent covers the bulk
  of tickets.
- For the training arm: course delivery and grading largely automate.

## The AI stack (2026)

Agentic coding for the platform · matching / screening agents · content agents for
curriculum · billing + scheduling agents · AI support agent.

## Human-in-the-loop — the supervisor role

- **Relationships on both sides** — talent trusts a person to represent them;
  clients trust a person to vouch for quality.
- **Quality curation** — the judgment of who is genuinely good is the product;
  a marketplace that lets bad talent in loses its trust premium.
- **Dispute resolution.**
- **Certification credibility** — a certificate is worth only what the issuer's
  name is worth.

## What blocks the last mile to 100%

The product *is* humans and trust. Relationships do not automate. Quality judgment
about people is irreducibly human.

## Failure modes of the automated approach

The structural risk: the automation thesis eats its own market — if AI gets good
enough at the human-touch roles (RP acting, community leadership, moderation), the
labor demand this opportunity sells into shrinks. It is also a low-margin
people-business, and any marketplace faces the cold-start liquidity problem —
neither side joins until the other is already there.
