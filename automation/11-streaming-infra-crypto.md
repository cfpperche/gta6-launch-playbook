## Verdict — ~90% of *execution* automatable, same pattern as #9

A small basket (THETA / RNDR / LPT) with DCA entry and a defined exit is fully
bot-executable. The thematic link between GTA 6 and these tokens is *thin*, so what
matters — again — is the human judgment on whether the narrative holds, not the
execution. Automating the trade does not strengthen the thesis.

## Build phase — automating the strategy

- An agent codifies the basket, the DCA schedule, and the exit rules; backtests;
  and flags the thin GTA-6 linkage as a known weakness.

## Operate phase — automating execution

- The bot runs DCA, rebalances, and exits.
- A macro / narrative agent monitors crypto conditions and narrative strength.

## The AI stack (2026)

Crypto trading bots · on-chain + narrative-monitoring agents.

## Human-in-the-loop — the supervisor role

- **Judge the (thin) thesis** — is launch-week streaming demand actually a price
  driver for these tokens, or just a story?
- **Set the cap; read crypto macro.**

## What blocks the last mile to 100%

The thesis is the bet. Nothing mechanical fixes a weak premise.

## Failure modes of the automated approach

Slower-moving than memecoins — less catastrophic, also less upside. The thin
GTA-6 linkage means the "narrative" may simply never form, leaving a basket held
for nothing. Full crypto-macro correlation means a market drawdown drags the
basket down regardless of GTA 6.
